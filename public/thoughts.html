It is by chance we met, by choice we became friends. Friendship is a strange thing…we find ourselves telling eachother the deepest details of our lives, things we don’t even share with our families who raised us. But what is a friend? A confidant? A lover? A fellow e-mail junkie? A shoulder to cry on? An ear to listen? A heart to feel? A friend is all these things…..and more. No matter where we met, I call you friend. A word so small yet so large in feeling, a word filled with emotion. It is true that great things come in small packages. Once the package of friendship has been opened, it can never be closed. It is a constant book always written waiting to be read and enjoyed. We may have our disagreements, we may argue, we may concern one another…. Friendship is a unique bond that lasts through it all. A part of me is put into my friends. Some of it is my humour, some of it is my listening ear, some of it is real life experiences, some of it is my romanticism, but with all it is friendship.
I strive to be the best person I can be. Sure, I have my faults and have failed on numerous occasions, but who hasn’t? Nobody is perfect, and those who think otherwise, obviously have their head in the clouds. That doesn’t mean you can’t strive for perfection, or possibly be somebody else’s idea of “perfect”. To me, all of my friends and family, in some way are “perfect”. They are “perfect” in their jobs, their surroundings and most importantly, by just being themselves. Some may struggle more than others, but it’s the way they pick themselves back up and emerge from their faults, that constitute “perfection” in my eyes.
I’ve been sitting here in the same spot for a while, bored out of my tree and contemplating this thing called “life”. What exactly constitutes a life? This may seem like a weird question to ask, and I know the logical answer would be “to be amongst the living”, or somewhat. I mean, I know you don’t have to be in the here and now to be considered having a “life”, there have been a lot of people, animals and “things” that have come and gone in this world, that at some point had a “life”. But what else constitutes a “life”, other than breathing? Do you have to have a high paying job? Be drop dead gorgeous? Have lots of friends? or Smarts even? I ask this, because I am constantly hearing people say the phrase “Get a life” to other human, breathing beings and I am somewhat confused by this whole expression. Do these people not think they are talking to an actual person with a “life”? Am I just that stupid to think that every living creature on the face of this planet, in the here and now or those that have passed, do in fact have a “life” and it’s completely up to them in how they live their lives?
I honestly wish sometimes I didn’t go through life wearing my heart on my sleeve. I tend to develop relationships with people far too easilly and I end up getting hurt in the long run. I let my emotions take over my mind and body and before I know it, I’m invested in all these friendships that mean way more to me than they do to the people I have grown to love in some way. To some, this may not be a bad quality to have in life….but to me I find it makes life that much harder just knowing that I will probably more than likely get hurt in the end. I’m not saying I don’t have wonderful friends who I know would do anything for me, because I do….Probably more than I can count, which is awesome….I love each and every one of them dearly. But at the same time, it doesn’t take the pain away knowing that the other people in my life I have grown to love with all my heart don’t return the same feelings for me. I know I may not be the smartest or prettiest girl, but I believe I make up for all of that with my compassion for others and I honestly wish people would see that not everyone they meet in life is going to turn into a bad apple on them. Maybe that’s part of the problem….. maybe some of these friends I have grown to love have just been hurt too much in the past and don’t want to take the risk of opening up in fear that I too will turn on them in some way. What these people need to realize though is I have never and will never turn my back on someone who needs me as much as I need them. That’s what friendship is all about.
Respect is basically an attitude that is shown towards any individuals’ feelings or interest. It is acknowledging another’s feelings in any kind of relationship. It may not necessarily be feelings shared between individuals but could also be referred to animals, groups or between countries. It implies to the phrase “treat others the way you want to be treated.” I believe everyone deserves to be respected, especially celebrities. Imagine going through life in their shoes for a change. Always having people follow them around everywhere they go, taking pictures, asking for autographs… It must get quite exhausting! I know if it were me, I would at least hope there would be some decent people out in the world that would allow me some privacy and much deserved respect. I’m not saying I’m perfect in any regards to this very subject. I do try my best to give as much respect to everyone I know and talk to on a daily basis, but are there times I wish I had not said things and maybe disrespected someone that I had no intention of doing? Yes, and I’m not proud of that, but everyone makes mistakes, and I’m willing to learn from those mistakes and hopefully become a better person because of it. Please just learn to respect everyone around you and treat them as you would want to be treated. This world would be a much better place :)
Tattoos are more than just pictures, or symbols plastered on peoples bodies. Tattoos are a form of art that if done correctly and at a tasteful level, can actually look quite amazing and attractive to ones eye. I don’t necessarily agree with plastering your body with tattoos, but one or two, in areas of the body that maybe aren’t always noticeable by everyone in sight is not a bad thing by any measure. I believe tattoos should have meaning behind them, and not just for the sake of saying “Hey, look at me, I have a tattoo” just to be cool.. I am not against tattoos in any way.. I do have one myself… It is a multicoloured butterfly on my right shoulder.. The reason I picked a butterfly is not because it is “cute” or the normal type of tattoo a girl would get….lol.. I chose a butterfly because to me it represents new life, in the present, as well as the “after life”. Just like a caterpillar eventually turns into this beautiful creature over time, and is given a second chance at life…I believe when a person passes from this world, they too are given a second chance at living in a place more beautiful than any one of us could ever imagine possible.
